About company

National Power Company Ukrenergo is a private joint stock company with 100% state-owned shares, managed by the Ministry of Energy of Ukraine.
Ukrenergo is a certified in accordance with the ISO model and in compliance with the EU’s Third Energy Package transmission system operator of Ukraine with the functions of operational and technological control of the Integrated Power System of Ukraine (IPS), transmission of electricity via trunk power grids from generation to distribution networks, as well as commercial metering administrator and settlement administrator in the electricity market of Ukraine.

Ukrenergo ensures:

  • the balance of production and consumption of electricity and capacity in the power system in real time;
  • the operation and development of trunk and interstate power grids;
  • the parallel operation of the power system of Ukraine with the power systems of neighbouring countries;
  • the technical possibility of export / import of electricity to 4 countries of the European Union and neighbouring countries.

NPC Ukrenergo’s organisational structure* is based on the regional principle and consists of 4 territorial administrations (Northern, Southern, Eastern and Western) which include 15 regional grid maintenance centres (RGMC). There are 6 regional dispatch centres (RDC) performing dispatch control functions. There are also two specialized separated subdivisions: Budivnytstvo i remont (Construction and Repair) and Ukrenergoservice responsible for logistical and service support of Ukrenergo.

Our personnel of above 7 thousand employees maintain unique high-tech equipment of 103** substations of 220-750 kV and over 19 thousand kilometers of trunk and interstate transmission lines, which annually transmit hundreds of billions of kilowatt-hours of electricity from generation to distribution networks.

Ukrenergo builds new substations and transmission lines, reconstructs the operating ones to enhance the efficiency and reliability of power grids, ensure additional nuclear generating capacity and RES integration into the power system, and provide technical compliance with the standards and requirements of ENTSO-E. Annual investments reach up to UAH 5 billion.

The loan portfolio of Ukrenergo amounts to 1.4 billion euros.

Mission of company

Ukrenergo is a certified transmission system operator synchronously interconnected with ENTSO-E Continental Europe, holding leading positions among Eastern European TSOs in terms of effectiveness, reliability, energy security level and technological development. Ukrenergo serves as a platform for implementation of new technologies in the power sector (smart grid, demand response, V2G etc.).

Mission

The creation of conditions for the sustainable development of the Ukrainian economy by means of ensuring the functioning of a competitive, transparent and liberal electricity market, single with the EU countries, and providing equal opportunities to all market participants, including renewable energy sources, regarding unrestricted continuous access to the transmission system.

Slogan

As the power system operator, we control energy every single moment. We live and work in the era of rapid changes, so we have to change ourselves. The slogan of Ukrenergo – Driving the power of changes – reflects our aim to be an example of changes in Ukraine.

Strategic goals for the next 10 years:

  1. To ensure long-term reliable operation and development of the transmission system;
  2. To integrate the electricity market of Ukraine into the pan-European electricity market and synchronise the operation of the IPS of Ukraine with the ENTSO-E Continental Europe power system;
  3. To ensure stable and balanced operation of the IPS of Ukraine;
  4. To minimise the cost of company services in the long run.

A few words about the Ukrenergo without the numbers and unnecessary information, and during the war

The personnel of Ukrenergo fights with enemy at energy front and at the real front the same as the army of Ukraine.

The first photo in this post shows the moment when the Ukrenergo repair crew is repairing a severed high-voltage line in the Chornobyl exclusion zone in March, while the occupiers are causing new damage. Our people worked in such conditions for weeks to restore power to the Chornobyl NPP, spent nuclear fuel storage and other special facilities.

At that time, the occupiers allowed the brigade to have one phone for everyone, in order to take pictures of the work done and report that they had left the occupied territory. But it was possible to call only after the boys entered the territory controlled by the Armed Forces of Ukraine.
In the conditions of war, any random trifle can override all agreements "from above" about "green" corridors, military escort, etc. During one of the first trips to the occupied territory, despite a whole bunch of approvals of routes, lists of people, code words and rules, at one of the checkpoints, the occupiers pulled the brigade out of the car and put them face down on the ground. They were angered by the GPS beacon. Probably, like asphalted roads in the villages, washing machines and computers in every Ukrainian house, this seemed like an unprecedented luxury to the Russians: they could not believe that a simple repair pickup could be equipped with GPS.
The occupiers interrogated the boys, shot over their heads, and stole tools and grounding from the car, and were very happy to get away with a chainsaw. In the end, after negotiations, our people were released. In the future, all repairs to the Chernobyl zone were planned as a high-level special operation to protect people's lives.
In such conditions, Ukrenergo electricians had to examine more than 100 km of lines, find damage and reliably eliminate them. While repairs were being made in one place, shelling tore the line in other places. But, as Serhiy Shikeryavy says, at that time our guys did not see obstacles, but only their goal - to restore power to the Chernobyl nuclear power plant and spent nuclear fuel storage facilities. The residents of the city of Slavutych (about 25,000 residents in peacetime), who were also without electricity all this time, were also waiting for their help.
Now the Chernobyl nuclear power plant is reliably powered up. And this is only a part of the history of those days. The further from those events and the closer to our Victory, we will be able to tell more.

They saved the world from Chornobyl number 2.
